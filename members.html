Postdoctoral Researchers

Rafał Kurleto

ARPES on Strongly Correlated Materials

Rafał graduated with his PhD from Jagiellonian University in Kraków (Poland) in 2020, where he investigated cerium based heavy fermion materials. His main background is in photoelectron spectroscopies (ARPES, XPS, UPS), and thermal/transport measurements. He joined Dan Dessau's group in the beginning of 2021, where he studies the electronic structure of strongly correlated systems (topological kagome magnets, unconventional superconductors) using ARPES. In his spare time Rafał enjoys the Colorado outdoors.

  • rafal [dot] kurleto [at] colorado [dot] edu

  • Duane Physics G229

Dushyant Narayan

Device physics and ARPES on Strongly Correlated Materials

Dushyant graduated from The University of Texas at Dallas in 2014 with a B.S. in physics minoring in computer science. He is involved in efforts to develop novel device technologies using a variety of cutting edge spectroscopic techniques. He also studies quantum materials hosting interesting phases such as charge-density waves and electronic nematicity. Dushyant enjoys tennis, working on his car, and tinkering.

Garrison Linn

Ultrafast Optics and ARPES on Strongly Correlated Materials

Garrison is the fourth of five sons. It was his eldest brother, William, that first introduced him to Quantum Mechanics in the summer before college. This set Garrison on a course to complete a B.S. in physics at Birmingham-Southern College followed by an M.S. in physics at the University of Alabama at Birmingham. His master's research was on terahertz time-domain spectroscopy of 2D electron gases in high magnetic fields in David Hilton's group. He joined Dan Dessau's group in 2018. Garrison loves snowboarding, mountain biking, rock climbing, scuba diving, and learning new things.
